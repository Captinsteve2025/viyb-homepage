## ğŸ§  AI Scoring Algorithm

The AI analyzes each yacht against **50+ detailed criteria**, including:

### **Hull & Structure**
- Material (fiberglass, aluminum, steel, wood, carbon fiber)
- Hull condition (excellent, good, fair, poor)
- Deck condition
- Through-hull count
- Keel type (full, fin, wing, centerboard)
- Draft measurement

### **Engines & Propulsion**
- Engine type (diesel preferred for liveaboard)
- Engine hours & maintenance records
- Propeller type (fixed or folding)
- Fuel capacity & range under power

### **Essential Systems - Power & Energy**
- Solar panels (capacity in watts, condition, mounting)
- Wind generator (output, noise level)
- Battery bank (capacity in Ah, type: AGM/lithium/gel, age)
- Inverter/charger (capacity, condition)
- Shore power (available outlets, capacity)
- Generator (hours, fuel type, maintenance history)

### **Water Systems**
- Watermaker (capacity gal/hr, age, service history)
- Water tanks (total capacity, material, condition)
- Water filtration (drinking water system)
- Hot water heater (capacity, fuel type)
- Pressure water system (pumps, accumulator tank)

### **Sailing Equipment**
- Furling systems (in-mast/in-boom for main, roller for headsails)
- Sail inventory (main, genoa, jib, storm sails, spinnaker - condition and age)
- Rigging (standing rigging age, running rigging condition)
- Autopilot (brand, condition, below-deck or wind vane)

### **Navigation & Electronics**
- GPS/Chartplotter (screen size, age, mapping)
- Radar (range, open array or dome)
- Depth sounder (transducer location)
- Wind instruments (speed, direction)
- VHF radio (DSC capability, handheld backup)
- SSB/HAM radio (for offshore)
- Satellite communication (phone, weather)
- AIS (transceiver or receiver)
- EPIRB (registration, battery expiration)

### **Computer/Connectivity**
- WiFi/cellular booster (range extender)
- 12V outlets/USB (charging stations)
- Computer navigation (backup systems)

### **Dinghy & Tender**
- Dinghy type (hard bottom inflatable, rigid, size)
- Dinghy davits (capacity, swing-away type, condition)
- Outboard motor (HP, fuel type, 2-stroke or 4-stroke)
- Dinghy storage (davits, on deck, towed)

### **Safety Equipment**
- Life raft (capacity, last service date)
- Life jackets/harnesses (quantity, condition)
- Fire extinguishers (number, type, inspection dates)
- Bilge pumps (manual and electric, high-water alarms)
- Emergency steering (backup tiller)
- Jack lines & tethers (for safety on deck)

### **Comfort & Liveability**
- Cabin layout (berths, privacy, storage)
- Headroom (adequate standing height)
- Storage (lockers, lazarettes, hanging lockers)
- Workspace (galley size, nav station)
- Ventilation (hatches, dorade vents, fans)
- Heating (diesel heater, reverse-cycle AC)
- Air conditioning (capacity, efficiency)
- Insulation (hull insulation for temperature control)
- Fans (12V fans throughout)
- Refrigeration (12V or engine-driven)
- Freezer
- Marine head (manual or electric, condition)
- Shower (hot/cold water, sump pump)
- Gray water system (tankage or direct discharge)
- Holding tank (capacity, gauge, deck pump-out)
- Entertainment system (TV, stereo, speakers)
- Reading lights (adequate lighting)
- Privacy (separate cabins if needed)
- Guest accommodation (v-berth or salon conversion)

### **Nice to Have Equipment**
- Bow thruster
- Swim platform
- Bimini/dodger (sun/weather protection)
- Canvas condition (enclosures, covers)
- BBQ/grill (rail-mounted)
- Washing machine (12V or 110V)
- Desalination system backup (manual watermaker)

### **Legal & Administrative**
- Title/documentation (clear ownership)
- Registration (current, transfer requirements)
- Survey (recent marine survey, under 1 year ideal)
- Insurance (insurability, costs)
- Lien search (ensure no outstanding liens)

### **Financial Considerations**
- Year of manufacture (affects value and systems)
- Brand/builder reputation (resale value, parts availability)
- Market value (comparable boats, pricing trends)
- Refit history (major upgrades, maintenance records)
- Slip/mooring fees (current and future costs)

### **Location & Cruising**
- Current location (haul-out accessibility)
- Cruising ready (immediately vs. needs work)
- Blue water capable (offshore construction standards)
- Shallow draft (for coastal/ICW cruising)
- Marina vs. anchoring (suited for your plans)

### **Maintenance Access**
- Engine room access (critical for liveaboard)
- Service accessibility (can you reach systems?)
- Spare parts availability (for systems and engine)
- DIY-friendly (systems you can maintain yourself)
